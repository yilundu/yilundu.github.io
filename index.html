I am a fourth-year PhD student at MIT EECS, advised by Prof. Leslie Kaelbling, Prof. Tomas Lozano-Perez and Prof. Joshua B. Tenenbaum. Previously, I obtained my bachelor's degree from MIT, was a research fellow at OpenAI, an intern at Deepmind and FAIR, and got a gold medal at the International Biology Olympiad.

My research interests lie in developing machine learning tools to create autonomous embodied agents. Two major challenges presented in the embodied setting include: high uncertainty and the rich combinatorial nature of the world. To tackle these issues, my research has focused on using energy-based models as a method to model probability distributions and uncertainty and to construct composable models that can flexibly adapted to new experience. I have also explored how this iterative energy optimization procedure can be used as an adjustable computational budget and as a means to combine existing large pretrained models. Embodied learning is multimodal, and I am interested in using neural fields as a tool to capture structure across modalities such as vision, text, sound, and touch. Finally I'm interested in broader applications of these tools to other domains such as computational biology.

Research Highlights
  • Generative Modeling: constructing generative models of the world's structure which may be combined and adapted.
  • Perception and Scene Understanding: inferring structured representations of the world for downstream embodied tasks.
  • Interactive Learning: building intelligent agents which may interact in the surrounding world.
