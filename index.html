I am a third-year PhD student at MIT EECS, advised by Prof. Leslie Kaelbling, Prof. Tomas Lozano-Perez and Prof. Josh Tenenbaum. Previously, I obtained my bachelor's degree from MIT, worked at OpenAI and FAIR, and got a gold medal at the International Biology Olympiad.

I am interested in constructing intelligent household agents which can accomplish common everyday tasks, such as fetching an object or making a cup of tea. To enable agents to operate from raw sensory inputs, I am interested in extracting representations of the surrounding world from perception, which may be used to facilitate both object manipulation and navigation in the world. To allow agents to generalize and adapt to novel situations and goals, I am further interested in generatively representing the structure of world, allow iterative inference on our world model at test time for better generalization. Finally, to construct agents that may incrementally acquire new skills and which can generalize to novel combinations of inputs, I am interested in constructing system that are composable and modular in nature. My research utilizes tools in energy-based modeling and implicit neural representations.

Research Highlights
  • Compositionality: constructing modular / composable models which enable combinatorical generalization, incremental learning, and controllability.
  • Perception and Scene Understanding: learning perceptual representations of the world that enable effective downstream manipulation and navigation.
  • Generative Modeling: constructing models of the world's' structure.
  • Interactive Learning: building intelligent agents which may interact in the surrounding world.
